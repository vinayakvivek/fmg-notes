## 1. Spermatogenesis

### Pathway

```
Spermatogonium (2n)
      │  ← Mitosis
      ▼
Primary Spermatocyte (2n, Diploid)
      │  ← Meiosis I (REDUCTION DIVISION)
      ▼
Secondary Spermatocyte (n, Haploid)
      │  ← Meiosis II
      ▼
Spermatids (n)
      │  ← Spermiogenesis
      ▼
Mature Sperm Cells
```

- Each **primary spermatocyte** gives rise to **4 sperm**
- **Reductional division** occurs at the step: **Primary → Secondary spermatocyte** (Meiosis I)

### Sperm Structure

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Tail/Flagellum  │  Mid-piece      │    Head     │
│   (Axoneme)      │ (Mitochondria)  │ (Acrosome,  │
│                  │  (Centeriole)   │  Nucleus)   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Spermiogenesis

- Conversion of **spermatid → mature sperm**
- Involves: nuclear condensation, development of flagellum, acrosome

### Capacitation

- **Final maturation** of sperm that makes them capable of fertilising the egg
- Occurs during passage through the **female genital tract**
  - **Starts** at the cervix
  - **Maximum** at the Fallopian tube
- Time taken: **6–8 hours**

### Time Durations (High-Yield)

| Process | Duration |
|---|---|
| Spermatogenesis | **70–74 days** |
| Spermiogenesis | **16 days** |
| Transport (testis → ejaculatory duct) | **12–21 days** |
| **Total (approximate)** | **~3 months** |

> **Clinical correlation:** In a man with azoospermia who has completed hormonal treatment, retest sperm count after **80–90 days (~3 months)**.

## 2. Acrosomal Reaction

- Sperm must first **fuse with oocyte's membrane**
- Contents of the **acrosome** break the tough coating of the ovum (zona pellucida), allowing sperm to **penetrate the ovum**

## 3. Cortical Reaction

- Release of **cortical granules** → **Hardening of zona pellucida** → **Prevents polyspermy**

## 4. Oogenesis — Development of Ovum

### Pathway

```
Primordial Germ Cell
      │
      ▼
Oogonium (2n)
      │
      ▼
Primary Oocyte (2n)
      │  ← Arrested in DIPLOTENE PHASE of PROPHASE I
      │  ← Meiosis I completed by LH surge (prior to ovulation)
      ▼
Secondary Oocyte (n) + First Polar Body (n)
      │  ← Arrested at METAPHASE II
      │  ← Meiosis II completed only upon SPERM ENTRY (fertilisation)
      ▼
Ovum (n) + Second Polar Body (n)
```

### Key Arrest Points

| Stage | Arrested At | Completed By |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Oocyte | **Diplotene phase of Prophase I** | **LH surge** (prior to ovulation) |
| Secondary Oocyte | **Metaphase II** | **Sperm entry / Fertilisation** |

### At Ovulation

- **Secondary oocyte** (arrested in Meiosis I) is released from the ovary
- Released along with the **1st polar body**

## 5. Follicle Numbers & Sperm Production

### Follicles in Ovary

| Time Point | Number |
|---|---|
| 20 weeks intrauterine life | **6–7 million** (maximum number of eggs) |
| At birth | **1–2 million** |
| At puberty | **3–4 lakh** |
| Total follicles that ovulate in lifetime | **~400** |
| Remaining follicles | Undergo **atresia** |

### Sperm Production

| Time Point | Number |
|---|---|
| During embryogenesis | 3,00,000 / testis |
| At puberty | 600 million / testis |
| Production rate in adult | **100–200 million / day** |

## 6. Fertilisation & Implantation

### Journey from Fertilisation to Implantation

```
Fertilisation (at AMPULLA of Fallopian tube)
      │
      ▼
Pronuclear Embryo → Cell Division → 1-celled stage
      │
      ▼
MORULA (16 cells) ──→ Enters uterine cavity on DAY 4
      │
      ▼
BLASTOCYST
      │
      ▼
IMPLANTATION (Day 6–10, starts by Day 6)
```

> **Important:** Embryo enters the uterine cavity on Day 4 as a **MORULA** (not blastocyst).

### Implantation Details

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Begins with | **Zonal hatching** (zona pellucida disappears Day 5–6) |
| Site of implantation | **Upper posterior wall** of uterus |
| Type of implantation | **Interstitial** |
| Timing | **Day 6–10** (starts by Day 6) |

### Blastocyst Structure

```
              BLASTOCYST
       ┌──────────────────────┐
       │                      │
  Inner Cell Mass        Trophoblast
       │                      │
       ▼               ┌──────┴──────┐
    EMBRYO              │             │
               Syncytiotrophoblast  Cytotrophoblast
                  (OUTER)            (INNER)
                    │                   │
                Secretes hCG        Forms Villi
```

## Quick Revision Summary

1. **Spermatogenesis:** Spermatogonium → (Mitosis) → 1° Spermatocyte → (Meiosis I) → 2° Spermatocyte → (Meiosis II) → Spermatid → (Spermiogenesis) → Sperm. Each 1° spermatocyte → **4 sperm**.
2. **Capacitation** occurs in female genital tract (cervix → max at F. tube), takes **6–8 hrs**.
3. **Acrosomal reaction** breaks zona pellucida; **Cortical reaction** prevents polyspermy.
4. **Oogenesis arrest:** 1° oocyte at **Diplotene/Prophase I**; 2° oocyte at **Metaphase II**.
5. **Max oocytes:** 6–7 million at 20 weeks IUL; only **~400 ovulate** in lifetime.
6. **Fertilisation** at **ampulla**; embryo enters uterus on **Day 4 as morula**.
7. **Implantation:** Day 6–10, at **upper posterior wall**, type = **interstitial**, begins with **zonal hatching**.
8. **Blastocyst:** Inner cell mass → embryo; Trophoblast → syncytiotrophoblast (outer, secretes hCG) + cytotrophoblast (inner, forms villi).
